The frame

Most teams building AI-native products automate the wrong layer.

Renewcast has the right ML layer already. The 10% that has to be AI, per-asset accuracy against chaotic weather inputs, is where the moat sits. The 30% that is rule-based, market settlement rules, per-country dispatch logic, customer reporting templates, does not belong in the model. The 60% that is traditional code and data work, SCADA ingestion, NWP plumbing, drift monitoring, model registry, customer-specific configuration, should sit on a clean orchestration layer that any new engineer can read in a day. The short form is 60/30/10. The useful form is applied to your actual stack, per pipeline, per customer type.

60%

Traditional code & data

SCADA ingestion. NWP plumbing. Drift monitoring. Model registry. Per-customer configuration. Readable in a day.

30%

Rule-based logic

Market settlement rules. Per-country dispatch logic. Customer reporting templates. Outside the model, inside the rules.

10%

Genuine AI

Per-asset forecasts against chaotic weather inputs. Digital twin accuracy. The 7% nMAE and the moat behind it.

Eduba’s job on an engagement like this is to draw the line, write the pattern down, and hand it back so your team can scale against it. Each new commercial customer should cost fewer engineering hours than the last, not the same.
